## Phrase 1: push back on

**Russian:** возразить против, отрицательно отреагировать
**Domain:** stakeholder-pushback
**Status:** #semi-active

### Context
Use when disagreeing with stakeholder requests, timelines, or demands. Direct but professional way to express disagreement or concern.

### Examples

**Natural spoken:**
> "I need to push back on that timeline given the technical complexity."
> "Can I push back on this requirement? It might create scope creep."

**Too formal — avoid:**
> "I would respectfully disagree with said timeline."
> "I must express my reservations regarding this requirement."

**Confident version:**
> "I'm going to push back on this. It's not realistic."

**Softer diplomatic version:**
> "I'd like to push back slightly on this timeline if possible."

## Phrase 2: loop in

**Russian:** подключить к обсуждению, привлечь к обсуждению
**Domain:** stakeholder-pushback, collaboration
**Status:** #active

### Context
Use when you need to involve someone in a discussion, decision, or meeting. Common in collaborative environments.

### Examples

**Natural spoken:**
> "Let's loop in engineering on this decision."
> "Should we loop in the CTO before committing?"

**Too formal — avoid:**
> "We ought to involve the engineering personnel."
> "It would be prudent to consult with the CTO."

**Confident version:**
> "We need to loop in engineering. They'll have good input."

**Softer diplomatic version:**
> "Maybe we should loop in David? He knows this area well."

## Phrase 5: find middle ground

**Russian:** найти компромисс, найти золотую середину
**Domain:** stakeholder-pushback, compromise
**Status:** #passive

### Context
Use when seeking compromise between conflicting positions. Important for maintaining relationships while still addressing concerns.

### Examples

**Natural spoken:**
> "Let's find middle ground between these two approaches."
> "Can we find middle ground on the timeline?"

**Too formal — avoid:**
> "We must seek a compromise between these positions."
> "It is essential that we locate an intermediate solution."

**Confident version:**
> "We can find middle ground. Both sides have valid points."

**Softer diplomatic version:**
> "Maybe we can find middle ground? I want to address both concerns."
