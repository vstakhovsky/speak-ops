## Meeting Notes

**Date:** June 28, 2025
**Attendees:** PM (Sarah), VP of Sales (Mike), Engineering Lead (David)

### Discussion

**Mike:** We really need to get this feature into the Q2 release. Sales is asking for it daily and it's becoming a competitive issue.

**Sarah:** I understand the urgency, Mike. But looking at the technical complexity, I need to push back on that timeline. Engineering is already at capacity with the current roadmap.

**Mike:** What if we added more resources? Can we pull in contractors?

**David:** That would actually slow us down. Onboarding contractors takes time we don't have. Plus, integrating them into the codebase would create technical debt.

**Sarah:** Let's loop in the CTO and get her perspective on this. She might have ideas we haven't considered.

**Mike:** Fine, but we need to move quickly on this. The market isn't waiting.

**Sarah:** Understood. Let's table this discussion until Thursday when we have the CTO's input. We can revisit it then with a fuller picture.

**David:** Sounds good. In the meantime, we should prioritize the existing Q2 features and not add new ones.

**Sarah:** Agreed. Let's align on the current roadmap and stick to it for now. We can double down on the top 3 features and get those done well.

**Mike:** Alright. But I want to find middle ground where we can at least commit to a timeline for this feature, even if it's not Q2.

**Sarah:** Fair point. Let's shelve the feature for now but put a stake in the ground for Q3. We'll revisit it in late Q2 and lock down a Q3 timeline.

**Mike:** Deal. I can live with that.
