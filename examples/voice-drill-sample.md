## Drill: Stakeholder Pushback Meeting

### Target Phrases

1. push back on
2. loop in
3. align on
4. table this
5. find middle ground

### Scenario

You are a Product Manager in a meeting with the VP of Sales (Mike). Mike is pushing hard for a new feature to be added to the Q2 roadmap, but Engineering is already at capacity. You need to handle this pushback appropriately while maintaining the relationship.

### Roles

- **AI (ChatGPT Voice):** VP of Sales (Mike) — Pushing hard for the feature
- **User:** Product Manager — Managing the pushback

### Conversation Setup

**Start:** Mike opens the conversation by pushing for the feature aggressively.

---

## ChatGPT Voice Prompt

```markdown
You are the VP of Sales (Mike). I am a Product Manager.

**Scenario:** I'm in a meeting with you where you're pushing hard for a new feature to be added to the Q2 roadmap. However, Engineering is already at capacity with the current roadmap. I need to handle this pushback appropriately.

**Target phrases I need to use:**
- push back on
- loop in
- align on
- table this
- find middle ground

**Conversation rules:**
1. Keep your turns short (2–3 sentences max)
2. Create natural situations where I would use the target phrases
3. Be somewhat aggressive but professional (you're a VP after all)
4. If I don't use a target phrase within 1–2 minutes, gently steer the conversation that way
5. Correct ONLY major mistakes (grammar, clearly wrong usage)
6. If I make a major mistake:
   - Stop me gently
   - Give me a better spoken version
   - Ask me to repeat the better version aloud
7. Don't correct minor differences in phrasing
8. After 5 minutes, end with:
   - Which phrases I used well
   - Which phrases I missed or used incorrectly
   - Specific feedback on naturalness

**Start now. Open by pushing hard for the feature:**

"We need to get this feature into Q2. Sales is asking for it daily and it's becoming a competitive issue. What can you do to make this happen?"
```

## Expected Flow

**How the conversation might go:**

1. **Mike (AI):** Pushes for feature — "We need this in Q2. Sales needs it."

2. **You:** Use **push back on** — "I need to push back on that timeline given the technical complexity."

3. **Mike (AI):** Pushes back — "What if we add contractors?"

4. **You:** Use **loop in** — "Let's loop in the CTO and get her perspective."

5. **Mike (AI):** Agrees reluctantly — "Fine, but we need to move quickly."

6. **You:** Use **table this** — "Let's table this until Thursday when we have her input."

7. **Mike (AI):** Wants some commitment — "I need something to tell Sales."

8. **You:** Use **find middle ground** — "We can put a stake in the ground for Q3."

9. **Mike (AI):** Accepts — "Deal. I can live with that."

10. **AI ends with feedback on your phrase usage.**

---

## Tips for Success

- **Be direct but polite** — You're managing a VP, not a peer
- **Use phrases naturally** — Don't force them if the moment isn't right
- **Acknowledge their concern** — Show you understand Sales needs
- **Offer alternatives** — Don't just say no
- **Maintain relationship** — Disagree without being disagreeable
